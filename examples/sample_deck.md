# DevFlow — AI coding agent for legacy codebases (Seed)

## Deck

**Problem.** Enterprises sit on huge legacy codebases. Senior engineers spend ~60% of their time just reading old code before they can change anything.

**Solution.** DevFlow is an AI agent that reads your whole monorepo, proposes a change, and opens the PR.

**Traction.** Live for 8 months, $420K ARR. $340K of that is our largest customer, MegaCorp (a Fortune 500 manufacturer); the rest is 5 small teams on self-serve.

**Unit economics.** Gross margin ~38% (heavy inference cost, plus 2 delivery engineers dedicated to MegaCorp).

**Market.** TAM $50B — ~30M software engineers worldwide × $1,500/seat/year.

**Team.** Both co-founders are ex-MegaCorp engineers. A well-known industry advisor has verbally agreed to join for 1% equity; we'll paper it after we raise.

**Ask.** Raising $4M for 18 months of runway, mostly to grow the sales team and land more large accounts.

## Founder note

Our strongest moat is the deep integration with MegaCorp — they can't rip us out. Growth comes from big-account word of mouth, so we plan to copy the MegaCorp playbook onto more enterprises. Inference cost is high today, but it'll dilute at scale. The advisor equity is verbal for now; everyone involved is a friend, so a handshake is fine.
